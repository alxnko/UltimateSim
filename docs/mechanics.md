# Boundless Sovereigns: Simulation Mechanics

This document details the exact, unabstracted systems that power the 6 Pillars of the Total Simulation in Boundless Sovereigns. No mechanic should be abstracted away if it can be simulated by the ECS.

---

## 1. Genesis & Geography

The world begins as a blank slate.

- **Procedural Biomes:** The engine generates an initial world map with varying climate zones, resource deposits (iron, stone, forests), and geographical barriers (mountains, rivers).
- **The Random Spawn:** Every NPC (and the player) spawns into the wilderness in small clusters—primarily family units. Every generated NPC has an autogenerated personality (e.g., Gossip, Loyal, Ambitious).
- **Settlement Birth:** NPCs wander based on personality/needs. When a cluster finds a location satisfying their "Need Vectors," they establish a village. Over time, these dots grow based solely on how many other wandering NPCs they attract through information flow.

## 2. Physical Infrastructure & World Scarring

The map is a participant that records history.

- **Desire Paths:** If hundreds of traders or migrating NPCs walk the same path frequently, the ECS records this traffic. Map tile textures physically change: Grass -> Dirt Path -> Packed Stone Road. Nature reclaims abandoned roads.
- **Resource Depletion:** Wood and stone are tied to local tiles. Over-harvesting to hastily build a city inevitably causes a crisis (e.g., heating shortage in winter), forcing the city to adapt, trade, or start a resource war.
- **The "Ruins" Cycle:** If a city’s economy collapses (starvation/war), NPCs migrate away. The city entity dies, but the map tiles retain their built state, turning into "Ruins." Decades later, wandering NPCs might re-settle them, using the existing foundations.

## 3. The Social Fabric

The core of the simulation is the web of obligations binding NPCs.

- **The Social Stack:** NPCs belong sequentially to a Family, Clan, Guild, City, and Country.
- **The "Hook" System:** Relationships are transactional. Helping an NPC grants a "Hook." Hooks are spent to force votes, extract secrets, or demand support.
- **Genetic & Social Legacy (Succession):** Upon death, the "camera" shifts to an heir. The heir inherits biological traits (strength), the powerful network of Social Standing (positive Hooks and inherited Debts/Blood Feuds), and Artifacts (e.g., "Sword of Bektur") that carry historical memory and grant "Auras of Legitimacy."

## 4. The Cognitive, Linguistic, & Memetic Engine

NPCs are physical observers; information is a massive, tangible resource.

- **The Linguistic Engine:** Language is dynamic. A village isolated for 100 years develops a _Dialect_; 300 years creates a _Foreign Language_. Information shared via gossip has a _Translation Penalty_ between languages, causing Misunderstandings leading to accidental wars. Dominant merchant guild languages naturally become the global _Lingua Franca_.
- **The Epistemological Layer (Truth Problem):** Initially, memory is oral (stored on NPCs; dies when they die). Advanced cities use Material _Ledgers_ which can be stolen, burned, or rediscovered. Conquerors can enact _Propaganda & Erasure_ by killing elders and burning ledgers, actively overriding the historical memory of the younger generation.
- **Individual Memory & Trade:** NPCs remember interactions (Hooks) based on personal goals. Information is a tangible, tradeable commodity in the ECS.
- **Leakage & Gossip:** Secrets shared with NPCs leak probabilistically based on the _Gossip_ trait or personal ambition.
- **Emergent Culture (Idea Virus):** Religions/cultures are shared belief components spread by _Charismatic_ NPCs. Geographical separation causes _Cultural Drift_. Massive societal trauma (plague) causes algorithmic _Traumatic Traditions_ (e.g., permanent Xenophobia).
- **The Vassal Safety Valve:** When a Clan becomes too powerful, the _Jealousy_ trait in rivals forces them to aggressively share negative secrets to destroy the monopolist's reputation.

## 5. Justice & Geopolitical Power

"Law" is a social construct dictated by power.

- **Contractual Law & Blackmail:** Breaking a Guild contract grants every member a Hook on the offender (blacklisting). Kings rule via Legitimacy Scores; if a deadly secret is gossiped about the King, the standing army revolts.
- **Blood Feuds:** Unresolved murders before a state exists trigger Blood Feuds. The ECS memory ensures grandchildren of rival clans still possess deep Negative Hooks, leading to endless frontier violence.
- **Organic Administration:** The NPCs holding the most influence (highest volume of powerful Hooks) naturally emerge as the "Administration" of a growing town. Cities confederate into Countries based purely on mutual economic/security needs.
- **State Capacity & Bureaucratic Friction:** Information latency prevents infinite expansion. Orders from the King are physical messages carried by NPCs that can be intercepted. _Administrative Decay_ causes distant governors to ignore distant kings, naturally fracturing massive empires.

## 6. Logistics, Economy, & Entropy

Negative feedback loops strictly punish unchecked growth or endless wealth.

- **Physical Logistics:** Grain in a desert city must be physically carried via Caravan. If Bandits kill the caravan, the grain is stolen, the city starves, happiness drops, and labor drains occur. Spoilage means grain rots and iron rusts.
- **The Debt & Interest Loop:** Countries and Guilds mint physical Coinage. If a King debases coinage (mixing lead with gold), NPCs detect it and cause organic Inflation. Extreme _Debt Traps_ allow Guilds to execute unpaid Hooks obtained as collateral, seizing political control without armies.
- **Price Normalization:** If grain prices skyrocket due to famine, Blacksmiths mathematically identify the profitability shift, emergentally switching jobs to farming, self-correcting the economy.
- **The Biological Pulse:** Winter spikes metabolic food consumption and slows movement, transitioning the game into an indoor political/scheming season.
- **Systemic Entropy (Plagues & Disasters):** Plagues spread along trade routes causing massive labor shortages. Surviving peasants demand higher wages, spontaneously forming revolutionary Trade Unions. Plagues leave behind _Immune Memory_, radically shifting global power balances the next time the algorithm triggers. Natural Disasters shift basic map parameters, forcing massive population resets.
